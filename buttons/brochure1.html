Interstellar Event Management Presentation

▋

Mission Brief

First-year students are summoned to the School of Cosmic Arts & Applied Eventcraft to present their original events — as if pitching to a council of professors from Hogwarts and a galactic management board.

Think of it as an advanced Event Management O.W.L. with a sci-fi twist.

The Trial

Each student team delivers a short, high-impact presentation about the event they intend to conduct during the semester. They must show that their concept can work in the real (Muggle) world — not just in magical theory.

  • Explain the event concept, target participants, and learning outcomes.
  • Walk the judges through how the event will actually run, step by step.
  • Demonstrate how technology and creativity will support smooth execution.
  • Convince the panel that their event deserves to be launched at Mystic Summit.

Artifacts of Power (Deliverables)

To prove their mastery, every team must conjure a complete event blueprint, combining magic and modern tech:

Spellbound Slide Deck
PPT Presentation

A clear, visually engaging PPT that summarises the event idea, flow, logistics, roles, and expected impact.

Moving Portrait Chronicle
Promo / Concept Video

A short video that introduces the event as a cinematic trailer or teaser, capturing the mood and purpose.

Wizarding World Brochure
Event Brochure

A brochure or one-page handout that explains the event to potential participants in simple, persuasive language.

Portal & Scrolls
Website & Registration

A simple event webpage and online registration form (Google Form or similar), with clear instructions and required fields.

Along with these, teams must present complete instructions for participants, resource requirements, and a practical timeline — everything needed for any other student to conduct the event without additional magic.

Judging Metrics (Effectiveness Spells)

Teams are judged not only on imagination, but on effectiveness — how convincingly they can move from idea to implementation. The panel will score each team on the following dimensions:

Clarity of Concept Is the event easy to understand?
Feasibility & Logistics Can it really be conducted on campus?
Creativity & Theme Is the idea fresh, engaging, and on-theme?
Communication & Delivery How strong is the presentation itself?
Use of Digital Tools PPT, video, website, and forms used effectively?
Teamwork & Role Clarity Are roles and responsibilities well defined?
Time Management Was the pitch kept within the time limit?

The final score reflects how convincingly a team can plan, explain, and actually run their event — as if they were launching it tomorrow.

Houses of Eventcraft

For flavour (and a little friendly rivalry), each event can align with a “house” — a blend of Hogwarts spirit and sci-fi mission profiles:

Stellar Gryffindor
Courage & Live Interaction

High-energy, stage-heavy events: debates, live games, public speaking, hackathons with live demos.

Quantum Ravenclaw
Ideas & Intellectual Design

Quizzes, research showcases, problem-solving challenges, and concept-driven workshops.

Nebula Hufflepuff
Collaboration & Inclusivity

Peer-learning events, mentoring circles, beginner-friendly skill labs, and collaborative projects.

Cyber Slytherin
Strategy & Ambition

Competitions with clear strategy, leaderboards, negotiation simulations, and design-to-win formats.

Outcome

By the end of Mystic Summit, students will not only have imagined an event — they will have designed a full, ready-to-launch experience with all the materials, links, and instructions needed to conduct it in reality.

Expectation: Walk in as first-year students; walk out as junior event managers armed with both wands and websites.

Hint for participants: Your PPT, video, brochure, and website are not decorations — they are proof that your idea can leave the page and live in the real world. Treat them like your personal Marauder’s Map for the event.

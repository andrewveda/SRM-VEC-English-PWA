TOPIC
Freedom, Democracy and the Cost of Silence
SUMMARY
My speech discussed how freedom is not permanently guaranteed in any democracy and can gradually decline when citizens become indifferent. I highlighted how lack of public questioning weakens accountability, allowing power to concentrate. Using global examples and democratic principles, I emphasized that active participation is essential to protect civil liberties.
CONCLUSION
Freedom is not sustained by constitutional documents alone but by citizens who stay informed, ask questions, and hold power accountable. When people choose comfort over responsibility, democracy quietly weakens. Protecting freedom is therefore not just a right, but a civic duty.

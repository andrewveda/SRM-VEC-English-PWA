Is our brain just another computer?
BB
Sunanda VK
EIE
Summary
When we compare the human brain to a computer, we often borrow the wrong language. That is where the confusion begins. We say the brain "stores" information and "retrieves" memories, just like a machine. But the brain doesn't work like a hard drive. Instead, when we recall a memory, we don't simply pull out a file, we re-experience the moment. The sights, the sounds, and the emotions come alive again. To show this, I involved my classmates in a small activity, helping them feel how vivid and personal memory truly is. Another fascinating aspect of the brain is its ability to grow and adapt. Unlike computers, the brain improves by forming new neural connections and even creating new neurons. This incredible flexibility allows us to adjust to new environments, learn continuously, and evolve with every experience. In short, the brain isn’t just a machine. It is a living, ever-changing system that reshapes itself with every thought and memory.
